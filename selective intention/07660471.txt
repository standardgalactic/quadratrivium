Summary: The existence theorem for replica-symmetry breaking (RSB) in the transverse ﬁeld Sherrington-
Kirkpatrick (SK) model is extended to the model with a general random exchange interactions. The
relation between the expectation value of the exchange interaction energy and the Duhamel correlation
function of spin operators can be obtained by an approximate integration by parts for general random
interactions. In addition to the Falk-Bruch inequality, these explicit evaluations enable us to prove that the
variance of overlap between two replica spin operators does not vanish under suﬃciently weak transverse
ﬁeld in suﬃciently low temperature. The absence of the ferromagnetic long range order is also shown to
distinguish RSB from the Z2-symmetry breaking.
