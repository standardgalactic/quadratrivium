The lecturer is Professor Michael Starbird. Michael Starbird is a professor of mathematics
and a university-distinguished teaching professor at the University of Texas at Austin. He received
his BA degree from Pomona College and his PhD degree in mathematics from the University
of Wisconsin at Madison. He is a member of the Academy of Distinguished Teachers and
has served as associate dean in the College of Natural Sciences at the University of Texas
at Austin. Professor Starbird is also a visiting member of the Institute of Advanced Study
in Princeton, New Jersey, a visiting associate professor at the University of California,
San Diego, and a member of the technical staff at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Pasadena,
California. Professor Starbird is the co-author of Coincidence, Chaos, and All That Math Jazz,
Making Light of Weighty Ideas. He is also the co-author of The Heart of Mathematics,
An Invitation to Effective Thinking, which won a 2001 Robert W. Hamilton Book Award.
Professor Starbird has won many teaching awards, including a Minnie Stevens Piper
Professorship, the Excellence Award from the Eyes of Texas, the President's Associates Teaching
Excellence Award, the Gene Holloway Award for Teaching Excellence, the students selected
Chad Oliver Plan 2 Teaching Award, and the Friar Society Centennial Teaching Fellowship.
Professor Starbird prepared the course guide that comes with these lectures. The course guide
includes a detailed outline of each lecture, a glossary, a timeline, biographical notes,
and bibliography. To get the most out of this course, you may find it useful to follow along
with the outlines or review them before or after each lecture.
