The lecture is Professor Arthur T. Benjamin.
Dr. Benjamin is Professor of Mathematics at Harvey Mudd College, where he has taught for more than 15 years.
He earned his Bachelor of Science degree from Carnegie Mellon University
and went on to earn his PhD in Mathematical Sciences from Johns Hopkins University.
Professor Benjamin's research interests include combinatorics, game theory and number theory,
with a special fondness for Fibonacci numbers.
He is co-editor of Math Horizons Magazine and co-author of Proofs That Really Count,
The Art of Combinatorial Proof, which received the Beckenbach Book Prize
from the Mathematical Association of America.
Arthur Benjamin is also a professional magician.
He frequently performs at the Magic Castle in Hollywood
and has given over 1,000 Mathemagic shows to audiences all over the world.
His techniques are explained in his book Secrets of Mental Math,
The Mathemagician's Guide to Lightning Calculation and Amazing Math Tricks.
Professor Benjamin has appeared on dozens of television and radio programs, including The Today Show, CNN and National Public Radio.
He has been featured in many publications, among them Scientific American, Discover Magazine and The New York Times.
In 2005, readers digest called him America's best math whiz.
Professor Benjamin's teaching has been honored by the Mathematical Association of America,
both with the Southern California Section Award for Distinguished College or University Teaching of Mathematics
and the Deborah and Franklin Temper Hymo National Award for Distinguished College or University Teaching of Mathematics.
Most recently, the MAA named Professor Benjamin the George Polnia Lecturer for 2006-2008.
Professor Benjamin prepared the course guide that comes with these lectures.
The course guide includes a detailed outline of each lecture, a glossary and a bibliography.
To get the most out of this course, you may find it useful to follow along with the outlines or review them before or after each lecture.
