These lectures are titled The United States and the Middle East, 1914-911, Part 1.
The lecturer is Professor Salim Yaqab.
Professor Yaqab is an assistant professor of American and international history at the University of Chicago.
He received his BA from the Academy of Art College and his MA at San Francisco State University.
He then went on to Yale University where he earned two additional master's degrees in philosophy and history and his PhD in American history.
He specializes in the history of American foreign relations, 20th century American political history, and modern Middle Eastern history since 1945.
Professor Yaqab is the author of Containing Arab Nationalism, the Eisenhower Doctrine, and the Middle East.
His dissertation of this work earned him the John Addison Porter Prize and the George Washington Eggleston Prize from Yale University.
Professor Yaqab prepared the course guide that comes with these tapes.
The course guide includes a detailed outline of each lecture, a glossary, a timeline, biographical notes, and bibliography.
To get the most out of this course, you may find it useful to follow along with the outlines or review them before or after each lecture.
