The lecturer is Professor Stephen L. Goldman.
Dr. Goldman is the Andrew W. Mellon Distinguished Professor in the Humanities at Lehigh University,
where he has taught for over 30 years.
He received his Bachelor of Science degree in Physics from the Polytechnic University of New York
and earned his MA and PhD in Philosophy from Boston University.
Prior to taking his position at Lehigh University, Professor Goldman taught at Pennsylvania State University
where he was co-founder of one of the first U.S. academic programs for the study of Science, Technology and Society.
He is also a co-founder of the National Association of Science, Technology and Society Studies.
A prolific author, Dr. Goldman has authored or edited eight books
and has an impressive list of scholarly articles and reviews to his credit.
The co-author of Agility in Healthcare, it was named Book of the Year by the Healthcare Information and Management System Society in 1998.
Professor Goldman is a co-founder of the Lehigh University Press
and for many years was the co-editor of its Research and Technology Studies series.
He has been a national lecturer for the Scientific Research Society, Sigma Xi,
and a national program consultant for the National Endowment for the Humanities.
Professor Goldman is a recipient of the Lindback Distinguished Teaching Award from Lehigh University.
Professor Goldman prepared the course guide that comes with these lectures.
The course guide includes a detailed outline of each lecture, a timeline, a glossary,
biographical notes and an annotated bibliography.
To get the most out of this course, you may find it useful to follow along with the outlines
or review them before or after each lecture.
